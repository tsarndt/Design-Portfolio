The 427 project is a 23-story luxury apartment complex located at 5th St. and Olive St. in downtown Los Angeles. Situated at the northeast corner of Pershing Square, this high-rise is in a unique position to be seen in its entirety from the park. As the landscape architects, we were tasked with the design of the streetscape frontage, second level amenity deck, and rooftop pool courtyard.

Due to the project location, we were able to develop two highly funcitonal schemes. The first was simply generated from the inspiration of the urban context - strong, simple geometries with reinforced visibility and clarity. Our second design was born out of a thorough understanding of the site history. 427 W. 5th St. was the former site of the Los Angeles Philharmonic Auditorium, whose interior design use of Art Nouveau styles inspired the more generous, free-flowing forms of our design.

Meléndrez - design, plans

Metropolis is a 6.33 acre mixed-use, high-rise development in the epicenter of Los Angeles. Located between the financial district and LA Live entertainment hub, Metropolis is seen as a key development in the ongoing revitalization of downtown Los Angeles.

Our task was to provide design solutions for Phase I of the project which includes a 38-story residential tower and a 19-story luxury hotel. By drawing inspiration from the urban context and the neighboring mountains, we were able to create a well-rounded Los Angeles experience for the entry plaza and the two elevated amenity decks.

The USC Village redevelopment project is the largest economic development proposal in South Los Angeles. It will include up to 350,000 square feet of retail shops, restaurants, full-service grocery store and gymnasium, while accommodating undergraduate and graduate student housing.

Our goal was to create a unique user experience to the students, faculty, and the surrounding community. By adopting the existing campus vernacular, we developed a product that represents the history and tradition of the University of Southern California.

A winner of the 2013 AIA LA Next LA Award, the Wilshire Grand tower will be a 1,100 ft. tall high-rise building located in the financial district of downtown Los Angeles. The building will be the home to mixed-use hotel, retail and office space and will be the country’s tallest skyscraper outside New York City and Chicago.

My team’s involvement in the project will be evident in three key spaces: the entry plaza, mezzanine, and the pool deck. The goals of the project were to embrace the urban context, create a sequence of inviting, human-scaled spaces, and to promote a multi-modal downtown.

As a nonprofit residential community, Century Villages at Cabrillo aims to break the cycle of homelessness. The Gateway is a four-story, 80 unit structure located in Long Beach, California that provides housing for veteran and non-veteran individuals, families, and children. The goal of this project is to aid in the transition of these once-homeless tenants back into society.

Our mission was to design three courtyard spaces that align with the overarching project goal. Using the notion of private, semi-private, and public spaces, we defined specific levels of interaction where users can comfortably embrace social relationships at their own pace.
